Carousel cards with interactive video thumbnail

The objective was to explore different possibilities to utilize live media for our event cards when it's available from our providers focusing on mobile platform with a hypothesis of increasing user engagement for visiting a certain event's detail page or placing a bet right away from the card.

Discovery

The scope of my discovery was the Event carousel and its cards featured on our Category and Most Popular pages.

As a first step I looked into different video aspect ratios and their usages on popular social media platforms and compared them to our current production- and proposed dimensions after our Design System updates.

Designing component variant proposals

Once I had the matching and most feasible aspect ratios I've created the carousel card variants based on our current production designs using different video sizes and layout permutations.

At this point the ongoing Design System initiative only covered the atomic level components so I've created this card molecule from scratch without any tokenization and such. It was already decided that we will move forward to a prototyping phase so I only designed for one brand in this step taking our two event phases (live & prematch) into consideration.

After showcasing the possible variants on a demo session for the stakeholders we narrowed down the scope for prototyping.

Hi-fidelity prototyping

At this time the video embedding in Figma was very rudimentary and our stakeholders wanted to test this component on an actual handheld device - not only as a desktop screen emulation - so as a part of my second discovery process I looked into the possibities of the Intersection Observer API and the Video Embed element and used my Angular-based application to build the prototypes for web browsers.

With URL-based feature toggling I've created four variants based on different thumbnail sizes and user controls to test the autoplay/autostop options for well developed markets. Plus an additional dark themed version to cover one of our Nordic brands.

Prototype evaluation & Conclusion

To summarize our possibilites and limitations I've created a pretty hefty presentation deck breaking down the variants including OS- and app-specific information about the autoplay functionality, native HTML Video element controls and comparison to our current implementation regarding interchangable media types.

Feel free to download the presentation, in a .key format but beware of the size because it contains videos :)

The conclusion by different stakeholders including members of our Offering team was to abandon the project because we were only receiving (affordable) live streaming material for low profile events and the development was not feasible at the time. Fortunately the quickly developing Internet connectivity in our key markets and the promising trends of E-Sports will turn this ship around anytime soon.
