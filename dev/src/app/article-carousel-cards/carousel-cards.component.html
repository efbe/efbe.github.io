Carousel Cards with Interactive Video Thumbnail

The objective was to explore various possibilities of live streaming within our Sportsbook product, with the hypothesis of enhancing user engagement by enticing them to visit a specific event's detail page or place a bet based on the information acquired from the displayed video content.

In this project, my role involved collaborating with the Frontend Tech Lead to explore available technologies, design potential variants, and craft high-fidelity prototypes for the selected ones.

Video Player Discovery

The scope of my discovery was to integrate video formats, supplied by our third-party providers, into the event carousel cards featured on our "Category" and "Most Popular" pages, with a specific focus on the mobile platform.

As a first step, I researched different video aspect ratios and their best practices on popular social media platforms. I then compared them to our current production and proposed Design System dimensions.

Designing Component Variant Proposals

After selecting the most feasible aspect ratios, I created carousel card variants based on our current production designs, incorporating different video sizes and layout permutations.

At this point, the ongoing Design System initiative only covered the atomic-level components. Therefore, I created this card molecule from scratch without any tokenization or similar processes. Since it was already decided that we would move forward with a prototyping phase, I designed for one of our brands, considering our two event phases (live and prematch).

After showcasing the possible variants in a demo session for the stakeholders, we narrowed down the scope for prototyping.

Hi-Fidelity Prototyping

At that time, video embedding in Figma was very rudimentary, and our stakeholders wanted to test this component on actual handheld devices, not just desktop screen emulations. As part of my second discovery process, I explored the possibilities of the Intersection Observer API and the Video Embed element. I utilized my Angular-based application to build prototypes for web browsers.

Using URL-based feature toggling, I created four variants based on different thumbnail sizes and user controls to test autoplay/autostop options for well-developed markets. Additionally, I developed an extra dark-themed version to accommodate one of our Nordic brands.

Prototype Evaluation and Conclusion

To summarize our possibilities and limitations, I created a comprehensive presentation deck that breaks down the variants. This includes OS- and app-specific information about the autoplay functionality, native HTML Video element controls, and a comparison to our current implementation regarding interchangeable media types.

The conclusion reached by various stakeholders, including members of our Offering team, was to abandon the project due to the availability of only (affordable) live streaming material for low-profile events and the infeasibility of development at the time. However, the rapidly developing internet connectivity in our key markets and the promising trends of eSports suggest that this situation may change soon.

Sharing Knowledge

I really enjoyed learning throughout the process, prompting me to share the knowledge with members of both the design and development teams. I crafted a second presentation solely focusing on the potential of the applied technology. This presentation includes examples utilizing the Intersection Observer API and specifics about how browsers on different operating systems render native HTML Video players.
