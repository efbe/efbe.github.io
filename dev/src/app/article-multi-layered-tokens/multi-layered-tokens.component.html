New Design System Component Building Approach

One of the main objectives of the Sportsbook Product Design Team was to redesign our leading online betting brand for the Nordic market. With a user base known for its conservatism, the goal was to modernize the user experience and elevate the brand's appeal.

To achieve this goal, we revamped our current color scheme logic and implemented a new design system component building method, along with an enhanced design token structure. The new approach and maintenance flow not only enable designers to directly integrate production code through Figma and GitHub, resulting in a faster time to market, but also play a crucial role in theme and tone switching within Figma. This allows us to experiment with and easily validate new design components across our different brands for various markets.

In this project, my role is to collaborate with the Principal UI Designer and Frontend Architect to establish the key artifacts. Additionally, I am responsible for onboarding and co-leading the UI Designers working on our Design System components.

Technical Discovery for Figma

The evolution of industry-standard design tools has progressed significantly in recent years.
We migrated our assets from Sketch to Figma a couple of years ago, and it was a challenging and costly process with minimal business value. Therefore, we had to be very cautious when selecting our foundations for the coming years.

I reached out to Figma's business representatives to obtain information about their plans for token management and plugin integration. Since their variable implementation ideas were quite basic at the time and the multi-brand support would have meant an additional cost for upgrading our entire organization's plan, I opted for Token Studio over their native variables after they assured us about the plugin support.

Design Token Architecture Enhancements

With the introduction of unified tokens, our main goals were cost-effectiveness and flexibility. To simplify component creation and UI theming, we reduced the number of structural and surface-related tokens. For added flexibility, which is often requested by our B2B partners, we increased brand-level properties, including so-called "product" background and complementary text colors, border radii, and richer typographical elements.

Based on the characteristics of cascading stylesheets responsible for rendering UI components in web browsers and utilizing the different token group levels provided by the Token Studio plugin, I've designed a brand new semantic 3-level token system.

Global structural tokens are shared across all brands and are responsible for spacings and sizings throughout the entire application. Brand-level tokens are used for both light and dark themes, while theme-specific adaptive tokens store surface and text color values for each brand's light or dark variations.

I also introduced a set of composite tokens for each respective level. For instance, typographic tokens are created at the brand level from global variables (such as line height) and brand-specific properties (like font family). Theme-specific text colors are interpolated with master opacity tokens to ensure consistency.

Taking our Color System to the Next Level

Originally, our products used a single tone for surface colorsâ€”either light or dark neutrals with the corresponding text colors, along with their respective brand colors. However, this new token structure has enabled us to support duo-tone themes.

We introduced a mirroring logic for the surface colors, consisting of 5 shades each from a 20-step generated palette, with an additional shared color that connects the light and dark tones for certain components. All colors from this palette are added to the tokens at the brand level to preserve each step along with the logic between them. We reference them at the respective theme level.

Having 20 shades instead of just the 11 that are actually used provides us with flexibility in achieving proper contrast with brand colors.

Text colors are based on the darkest and lightest palette colors and are modified with global variables to create high, medium, and low emphasis, similar to Material Design's color system. With this approach, we managed to achieve AA compliance standards for our most prominent components for certain brands.

Redesigning Components with the New Token Set

We began field testing these new color tokens by redesigning a handful of our most common UI components. This allowed us to assess the flexibility of the custom properties and observe how quickly the Token Studio plugin renders updates across different brands and tones at various atomic levels. Don't forget that we did not have native Figma Variable support at that time.

In the tab component, the new border radius token, along with smarter usage and reduction of surface colors, provides more emphasis on the content. The new system of text and surface colors allowed us to remove the brand color from the title text while achieving an AA grade contrast ratio.

For our market selector, I began moving away from desktop-specific controls and started utilizing brand-level color tokens. The navigation chips offer a native mobile experience and reduce the need for custom UI components. They also help distinguish the selection from other options while the horizontal swipe interaction simplifies the UX by eliminating an extra step / layer from the z-axis compared to the original dropdown solution.

In case of the event result selector, the new border radius token and smaller gaps between the buttons led to better cohesion within an event's selections. With the reduction of the dimension tokens, the selection buttons now use a standardized height instead of a custom value.

From Design to Code

Once I confirmed that the components worked as expected in Figma, I presented the proof of concept to our key stakeholders, UI team members, and Frontend Architects. Together, we began setting up the deployment part of the token pipeline.

After the resolver was in place to translate our design tokens to CSS variables in the production code, we rolled out our very first token set to the aforementioned Nordic brand in March 2024. The plan for this year is to continue with the rest of our brands, which means more than 15 individual themes, along with their light and dark variants.

Regarding our Figma components, we began building on our existing Design System, redefining components with the new tokens to support the duo-tone appearance. Since we previously didn't have a standardized handover process, I suggested extending the scope of this project to include the standardization of our visual handovers to developers, product owners, and quality assurance personnel. To achieve this, I began experimenting with the EightShapes Specs plugin developed by Nathan Curtis, creating complex UI component specs along with all available variants of their subatomic particles.

The Sportsbook design team decided to start to use the plugin with a limited set of features, outputting basic visuals to reduce the delivery time of our first brands. As the next step, we are planning to introduce more features, including token consumption details, and a standardized format for generated specs to introduce a comprehensive, living documentation for our new duo-tone design system components.

Plans for the Future

With the new token architecture and color system, the UI design team is technically ready to upgrade our existing components and craft new ones as requested by our product backlog. In the upcoming months, we need to agree on a token documentation format with the Front End developers and Product Owners. Additionally, since the number of component upgrade requests has increased with the redesign initiative, we need to enhance our prioritization process and update our roadmap for the year.

In addition to following up on feedback regarding the new system, its documentation, and processes from all product development participants through regular designer conversations and workshops, product-development Slack channels, wiki page idea pools, etc., I started to draw up plans for the next year. This includes a Storybook integration MVP and the introduction of a more dynamic and automated color palette creation based on the OKLCH color namespace to enhance the elevation system of our UI components and speed up our component creation cycle.

With the help of this new approach, we can begin creating alternate token sets to experiment with existing product brands and study significant modifications, such as accessibility enhancements. These variant research efforts will enable us to introduce our product to new markets, thanks to the flexibility of our Design System components.

My long-term goal is to create an ecosystem and culture where designers can craft highly personalized or at least geo-location agnostic components. This will enhance the user experience and increase the company's overall relevance in our key markets.
